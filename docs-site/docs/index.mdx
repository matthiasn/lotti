## Not another machine for pretending everything went to plan

Lotti distinguishes the things you meant to do from the things that actually
happened. A task can be important without owning your whole day. A plan can be
rearranged without falsifying history. A time record can say, honestly, that
the meeting ran long and the good idea arrived while you were walking home.

That separation is the point, not paperwork. It gives you a record you can
trust when the week gets noisy.

:::note About the penguins (and other excellent nonsense)

The penguins, orbital habitat, sardines, and **Project Waddle** appear only in
this manual's fictional **Intergalactic Penguin Logistics** demo workspace.
They make the screenshots feel like one coherent story, while the controls,
flows, and product behaviour shown are the real Lotti app. Your own workspace
can be as ordinary—or as delightfully strange—as you like. Task cover art is
optional; a good visual metaphor can make a crowded week much easier to read.

:::

## AI is optional. Its consequences are not.

Lotti has no inference backend and no copy of your model input. With local
inference, your material can stay on your device. With a cloud provider, your
chosen context goes directly to that provider under **your** account and API
key. That makes the decision yours—but it also makes the due diligence yours.

<div className="manual-responsibility-grid">
  <div className="manual-responsibility-card">
    <strong>Privacy is a routing decision.</strong>
    <p>For sensitive material, run a capable model locally or choose a provider only after checking its retention, training, jurisdiction, and account settings. “Cloud” never automatically means “nothing is stored.”</p>
  </div>
  <div className="manual-responsibility-card">
    <strong>Energy is a routing decision too.</strong>
    <p>AI runs in physical places. Prefer a local setup or provider whose electricity, location, and carbon claims you can actually verify. A renewable-powered route can avoid outsourcing the health and climate cost of fossil-powered compute—especially gas-turbine generation—to communities with less power to refuse it.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="manual-responsibility-card">
    <strong>Human approval is a product rule.</strong>
    <p>Agents can analyse context and draft changes, but proposals wait for you. Lotti does not turn “helpful” into “unaccountable.”</p>
  </div>
</div>

The practical rule: if you cannot find a data and energy path you are happy to
stand behind, leave AI off. Lotti remains useful without it.

:::tip Start small

Create a couple of meaningful categories, one real task, and one Daily OS
check-in. Add more structure only when it earns its place; you are building a
record of a life, not feeding a productivity machine.

:::
