# Plan a day with Daily OS

Daily OS turns a spoken or typed check-in into an editable plan for one day. It
does not replace tasks or time records: it proposes when work could happen,
shows the plan beside what you actually recorded, and lets you reshape the
schedule by voice or directly on the calendar.

The examples use a deliberately busy synthetic day for a fictional Director
of Interplanetary Penguin Logistics. Your penguin situation may vary.

## Before your first check-in

Open **Settings → Daily OS** and choose an inference profile. The settings page
identifies whether the configured endpoint is on this device or remote. You can
also set the name used in the greeting.

In **Settings → Categories**, enable **Day planning** for the categories the
planner may use. Keeping that set intentional improves suggestions and keeps
unrelated areas out of the ritual.

## 1. Say what the day needs

Open **Daily OS**, select the date, and choose **Speak a check-in**. Mention
fixed commitments, outcomes, rough durations, energy constraints, breaks, and
anything that should not happen too late. Use **Type instead** when speaking is
inconvenient.

After recognition, review the complete transcript before continuing. Correct
names, numbers, and other mistakes in place.

Choose **Re-record** to start over or **Review** to continue. **Build my day**
remains unavailable until the initial matching pass has completed.

## 2. Reconcile what the assistant heard

Reconcile is the safety check between speech and action:

- **Matched** points to an existing task. Confirm that it is the task you meant.
- **New** is a phrase that can become work for today.
- **Update** proposes a change to an existing task.
- Confidence and time-anchor labels show where review is most useful.

The next section surfaces work already in progress, overdue, due today, or
recently missed. Decide what belongs today instead of importing the entire
backlog.

## 3. Judge the draft

Choose **Build my day** after the decisions look right. The first result opens
on **Agenda**. The capacity card answers “does this fit?” before the numbered
list answers “what matters?”

<ManualScreenshot

  caseId="daily-os/agenda"
  caption="The agenda adapts to the selected device layout and manual theme."
/>

Use the quick adjustments when the shape is wrong:

- **Too much** asks for a smaller plan.
- **Move lighter** moves demanding work away from low-energy periods.
- **Add buffer** creates breathing room.
- **Refine** opens a free-form check-in for another change.

Choose **Looks good** only when the draft is genuinely acceptable.

## 4. Compare the plan with reality

Switch from **Agenda** to **Day** for the calendar projection. On desktop, Plan
and Actual share one time axis; on a phone, swipe horizontally between them.
Planned blocks use a lighter treatment and recorded sessions are filled, so
drift remains visible without rewriting history.

## 5. Arrange the plan directly

Choose the four-arrow **Arrange** action above the timeline. Drag a block body
to move it; drag the top or bottom handle to change its start or end. Changes
snap to 15-minute increments and stay inside the selected day. Use **Undo** in
the confirmation message when the old slot was better.

<ManualScreenshot

  caseId="daily-os/arrange"

/>

The pencil action opens title, category, timing, and placement details. A
task-linked block keeps its task-owned title and category read-only; use **Open
task** to change those at the source. Its scheduled start and end remain
editable from Daily OS.

## Remember

- Tasks describe the work.
- The day plan describes intent.
- Time records describe reality.
- The assistant proposes; you decide.
