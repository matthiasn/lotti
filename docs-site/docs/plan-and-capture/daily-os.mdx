# Plan and close a day with Daily OS

Daily OS turns a spoken or typed check-in into a plan for one day. It does not
replace tasks or time records: tasks describe the work, the day plan describes
intent, and recorded sessions describe what actually happened.

The examples follow Director Aurora through a deliberately busy day at
Intergalactic Penguin Logistics. The planner protects a Project Waddle launch
review, moves sardine negotiations, and keeps a zero-gravity fish-feeder demo
clear. Your penguin situation may vary.

## Before your first check-in

Open **Settings → Daily OS**. Choose the inference profile that should plan
your days and enter the name Daily OS should use when addressing you. The
privacy notice identifies the selected provider and whether processing is on
this device or remote.

Then open **Settings → Categories** and enable **Day planning** only for the
categories the planner may use. An intentional allow-list keeps unrelated
tasks out of planning and prevents Daily OS from changing work outside those
categories.

## 1. Say what the day needs

Open **Daily OS**, choose the date, and select **Speak a check-in**. Mention:

- fixed commitments and deadlines;
- the outcomes that matter;
- rough durations and available capacity;
- breaks, energy constraints, and work that should not happen too late;
- anything already in progress or likely to carry over.

Choose **Type instead** when speaking is inconvenient. After recognition,
review the complete transcript and correct names, numbers, or other mistakes
in place.

<ManualScreenshot

/>

Choose **Re-record** to start over or **Review** to continue. You cannot build
the day until the matching pass completes.

## 2. Reconcile speech with real work

Reconcile is the safety check between what the assistant heard and what Lotti
will change:

- **Matched** points to an existing task. Confirm that it is the task you meant.
- **New** is a phrase that can become work for today.
- **Update** proposes a change to an existing task.
- Confidence and time-anchor labels show where review is most useful.

<ManualScreenshot
  alt="Daily OS reconciling a spoken check-in with existing and new Project Waddle work"
  caseId="daily-os/reconcile"
  caption="The orbital habitat inspection is matched to real work while new requests stay explicit decisions."
/>

Daily OS also surfaces work that is in progress, overdue, due today, or
recently missed. Decide what belongs today instead of importing the entire
backlog.

## 3. Judge the draft

Choose **Build my day** after the reconciliation decisions look right. The
result opens on **Agenda**. The capacity card answers “does this fit?” before
the numbered list answers “what matters?” Task-backed rows keep their real
category, status, estimate, and cover thumbnail.

<ManualScreenshot
  alt="A Project Waddle Daily OS agenda with capacity, task thumbnails, priorities, and category totals"
  caseId="daily-os/agenda"
  caption="Every task in the agenda keeps its own Project Waddle cover art, including completed work."
/>

Use the quick adjustments when the shape is wrong:

- **Too much** asks for a smaller plan.
- **Move lighter** moves demanding work away from low-energy periods.
- **Add buffer** creates breathing room.
- **Refine** opens a free-form voice or text adjustment.

Choose **Looks good** only when the draft is genuinely acceptable.

## 4. Review every proposed refinement

Refine never silently rewrites the schedule. Describe the change, then inspect
each added, moved, or dropped block with its old and proposed time and the
planner's reason. Accept or reject proposals individually, choose **Revert** to
discard the pending set, or keep talking to request another pass.

<ManualScreenshot
  alt="Daily OS proposing two reviewed time changes for sardine negotiations and the fish-feeder demo"
  caseId="daily-os/refine"
  caption="Sardine negotiations move later to protect Mission Control's launch review; the fish-feeder demo follows the new buffer."
/>

Only accepted proposals become part of the draft. **Looks good** exits the
review once the plan says what you intend.

## 5. Lock in the day

The **Lock it in** page gives the complete draft one final reading: order,
duration, outcomes, and remaining capacity. Hold the confirmation control for
a second to move the plan from drafted to committed. Keyboard users can focus
the control and press Enter or Space; assistive-technology activation confirms
it directly.

<ManualScreenshot
  alt="The Project Waddle draft and deliberate hold-to-confirm action on the Daily OS commitment page"
  caseId="daily-os/commit"
  caption="Eight pieces of work use 8h 45m of a 9h day, leaving a 15-minute margin before Director Aurora signs off."
/>

Committing locks in the plan's bones, not your ability to adapt. You can still
use **Refine** afterward, but proposed changes remain reviewable.

## 6. Compare the plan with reality

Switch from **Agenda** to **Day** for the calendar projection. On desktop,
Plan and Actual share one time axis. On a phone, swipe horizontally between
the planned and recorded lanes. Planned blocks use a lighter treatment and
recorded sessions are filled, so drift stays visible without rewriting
history.

<ManualScreenshot
  alt="Daily OS timeline comparing Project Waddle planned blocks with recorded time"
  caseId="daily-os/timeline"
  caption="The phone pages between planned and recorded lanes; desktop shows both on one time axis."
/>

Start and stop time recording from the linked task as usual. Daily OS reads
those records as reality; it does not manufacture completed work from the
plan.

## 7. Move, resize, or edit a block

Choose the four-arrow **Arrange** action above the timeline. Drag a block body
to move it, or drag its top or bottom handle to change its start or end.
Changes snap to 15-minute increments and stay inside the selected day. Use
**Undo** in the confirmation message when the old slot was better.

<ManualScreenshot
  alt="Daily OS arrange mode with move and resize controls on Project Waddle blocks"
  caseId="daily-os/arrange"
  caption="Arrange mode exposes the same direct move and resize actions on phone and desktop."
/>

The pencil action opens the block editor. A task-linked block keeps its
task-owned title and category read-only; choose **Open task** to change those
at the source. Its scheduled start and end remain editable from Daily OS.

<ManualScreenshot
  alt="Daily OS block editor for the zero-gravity fish feeder task"
  caseId="daily-os/block-editor"
  caption="The linked task owns its title and Penguin Operations category; Daily OS owns the 2:30–4:00 PM slot and planning reason."
/>

Standalone plan blocks can edit their own title and category because no task
owns that metadata.

## 8. Close out the day

After a committed day, choose **Wrap up**. Shutdown separates decisions that
should not be conflated:

- **What you did** lists completed work with actual tracked duration.
- **Carries forward** lets you schedule unfinished work for tomorrow, pick a
  date, or drop it.
- Metrics summarize focus time, flow sessions, context switches, and energy.
- A one-line reflection feeds the next draft.
- **What I've learned** lets you confirm or forget durable planner knowledge.
- **For tomorrow** preserves the concise handoff note.

<ManualScreenshot
  alt="Daily OS shutdown with completed Project Waddle work, carryover choices, metrics, reflection, and learned preferences"
  caseId="daily-os/shutdown"
  caption="Director Aurora completed the habitat inspection, launch review, and sardine deal; the fish-feeder demo and passenger question stay explicit carryover decisions."
/>

Choose **Save & close** to leave the review without declaring the day closed,
or **Close the day** when the carryover and learning decisions are ready.

## The ownership rule

- Tasks describe the work and own task metadata.
- The day plan describes intent and owns scheduled blocks.
- Time records describe reality.
- Planner knowledge records reviewed preferences for future days.
- The assistant proposes; you decide.
