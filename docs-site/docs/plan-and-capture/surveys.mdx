# Complete and revisit surveys

Lotti includes three fixed questionnaires: **CFQ-11**, **GHQ-12**, and
**PANAS**. Add the survey you use to a dashboard, then select the **+** action
on its chart whenever you want to record another response. The dashboard is
both the launch point and the place where completed scores become a trend.

The examples below launch the real PANAS task from the **Colony operations**
dashboard. The questionnaire and its navigation come from the same production
survey runner used by the app.

## Add a survey to a dashboard

Open **Settings → Definitions → Dashboards**, edit a dashboard, and add a chart
from **Surveys**. The available instruments are:

- **CFQ-11**, the Chalder Fatigue Scale;
- **GHQ-12**, the General Health Questionnaire; and
- **PANAS**, the Positive and Negative Affect Schedule.

Return to **Dashboards** and open the dashboard. The chart shows submitted
results for its instrument. Select its **+** action to begin a new response.
Each chart launches the matching questionnaire; there is no generic form that
changes its questions based on documentation data.

## Answer the current question

The instruction step explains the requested time frame and the meaning of the
answer scale. Select **NEXT** to start. PANAS then presents 20 affect words one
at a time and asks for one choice from **Very slightly or not at all** through
**Extremely**.

The other bundled surveys use their own fixed question and scoring definitions.
Choose the response that matches the wording and time frame shown by the
instrument; Lotti does not reinterpret or auto-fill answers.

## Submit or cancel deliberately

Continue through the completion step to submit. Lotti stores the raw task
result and its calculated score buckets as a survey entry in the Journal. The
dashboard reads those entries back into its chart, so another completed survey
adds another dated observation.

Closing the task asks whether to discard the current result. A cancelled survey
is not stored, including partially answered work. If the response matters,
finish the completion step before leaving the sheet.

## Understand the saved scores

The persisted entry keeps both the individual answers and the calculated
summary used by charts:

- **CFQ-11** produces its configured fatigue score;
- **GHQ-12** produces its configured general-health score; and
- **PANAS** produces separate **Positive Affect** and **Negative Affect**
  totals.

Open the corresponding Journal entry to inspect the saved survey summary. Use
the dashboard for change over time and the Journal for the dated source record.
Survey charts do not replace the questionnaire's instructions, and Lotti does
not provide a survey authoring system for changing these bundled definitions.
