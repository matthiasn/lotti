# Roadmap

Everything on this page is design intent, not a shipped feature; scope and
details can change before release.

## Check your understanding, not just your output

Learning quizzes turn your own tasks into quick comprehension checks. From
any task you will be able to tap **Check my understanding** and get a short
quiz tailored to that task's content, mixing multiple-choice questions with
open questions you can answer by typing or by voice. Grading is
conversational: when an answer is shallow or ambiguous, the AI asks a
follow-up before judging, then explains what you missed, grounded in the
task content the question came from. Each quiz ends with a score, a label,
and a gap list, and keeps its own timeline — questions, answers, follow-ups,
and grades — so you can reopen it from the task later. Grades are AI
feedback about one quiz, never a credential or a comparison with other
people, and quiz history stays private, syncable, and deletable like other
agent data.

The planned progression:

1. **Quiz any task** on demand, with a quick-check or deep-dive choice and
   fresh questions each time.
2. **More surfaces and gentle suggestions** — projects, days, and agent
   reports, plus an optional "Quiz yourself on this?" card with strict caps
   and disable controls.
3. **Consented workspace evidence** so engineering quizzes can draw on
   repository diffs and test summaries through a read-only integration.
4. **History insights** that surface recurring gap themes over time.

Questions and grades come only from content Lotti actually captured — thin
content yields fewer questions rather than filler, suggestions never run AI
before you accept, and quizzes never block completing a task.
