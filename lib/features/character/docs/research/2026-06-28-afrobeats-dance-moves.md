# Afrobeats dance moves — keying reference (2026-06-28)

Count-accurate authoring notes per move, for the catalog in
[CHAR-0001](../adr/CHAR-0001-dance-choreography-encoding-and-move-library.md).
Each move was researched independently (web sources cited per move). Frame
numbers map to the 32-frame / 2-bar phrase (main counts = frames 0,4,8,…,28;
"and"s = 2,6,10,…). Coordinates are **side-on screen space**; cats face
screen-right.

> Authenticity flag: these are freestyle social dances; no source publishes a
> literal frame chart. Per-region mechanics, signature poses, and origins are
> source-backed; exact frame placements are authoring inferences fitted to the
> 2-bar grid. Verify scrub/slide frequencies against reference video before final
> keying.

## Zanku / Legwork — LEAD HERO (featured: legs)

- **Origin:** Zlatan Ibile, Nigeria, 2018 ("Zanku (Leg Work)"; first seen in
  "Able God"). Name = "Zlatan Abeg No Kill Us"; chant "gbe body e". Builds on
  Shaku; ~120 BPM.
- **Engine:** continuous alternating leg-lift/stomp on the balls of the feet with
  a small hop on each plant (single-support, alternating). Footwork runs at
  **8th-note rate** → a foot event on every even frame. Easy/viral layer = low
  alternating lifts ("kill an ant"); show-off layer = high air-kicks.
- **Per-region:** *feet/legs* — percussive stomp on the plant; free leg peels up
  then drags/flicks down; the **air-kick** thrusts forward-and-up ("knock the
  door"). *Torso* — leaned back ~10–20°, braced. *Arms* — elbows ~90°, fists at
  the ribs/chest, piston in opposition to the legs. *Head* — chin dropped ("look
  at the floor"). *Tail* — whips on the kick.
- **Signature pose:** support leg planted on the ball, the **other leg kicked
  forward-and-up** (lower leg extended, foot pointed), torso leaned back, chin
  down, fists at chest.
- **Accent map:** loop bed = alternating lifts every even frame; **promote one
  count per phrase to the air-kick** — anticipation gather/weight-sink on the "&"
  (≈fr 22–24) → extension **peak** (≈fr 26) → 1–2 frame overshoot (≈fr 28).
- **2D:** the air-kick is *peak* in profile (keep literal); stylize roundhouse →
  forward sagittal arc; offset near/far legs in depth so the lift reads.
- **Effort:** Strong + Sudden + Bound engine; brief Free overshoot on the kick.
- **Sources:** Filter Free; OkayAfrica; The NATIVE; How-To-9ja; District234.

## Shaku Shaku — BASELINE POCKET (featured: legs/medium)

- **Origin:** Lagos street scene (Agege), Nigeria, ~2017–2018; popularized by
  Olamide ("Wo"), Slimcase, Mr Real. Absorbed Sekem foot movements + shoulder
  pumping; fed forward into Zanku. Read as a loose "drunken" gait.
- **Engine:** a **2-count half-gallop** alternating lead side (a *false /
  unfinished* leg-over — a skating skip, aborted mid-travel). One knee-dip per
  count (downbeat = bottom of the dip); weight crosses side-to-side every 2
  counts.
- **Per-region:** *feet/legs* — wide astride stance, low shuffly half-gallop.
  *Knees* — permanently bent (the posture spine). *Arms* — both forearms crossed
  in an **X** at chest/face height, **held** and bobbing with the bounce;
  occasional "generator-pull" crank (≈fr 12). *Torso* — slouched, leans toward
  the lead foot. *Head* — loose bob. Loop-cap quick squat-and-rise (≈fr 28).
- **Signature pose:** wide bent-knee astride stance + crossed-arm **X** at the
  upper chest/face + slouched torso + low head.
- **2D:** half-gallop reads great as a fore/aft skid; **raise the X and angle the
  near forearm across the jaw** so it still cuts the silhouette from the side;
  re-encode lateral weight-shift as fore/aft sway + bob; open to a shallow 3/4 if
  the wide stance collapses.
- **Effort:** sharp-percussive legs inside a loose/Free body, with a **bound,
  held** X up top.
- **Sources:** Legit.ng; The Guardian NG; Pulse NG; Madjack.

## Azonto — BACKUP + CALL-RESPONSE (featured: arms)

- **Origin:** Ghana (Ga/Kpanlogo lineage; precursor "Apaa" = mimicking a trade),
  ~2011–2012; broke out via Sarkodie "U Go Kill Me" (2011). ~118–128 BPM,
  off-beat accent.
- **Engine:** a **container** — a continuous bent-knee 2-step bounce (knee swivel
  every beat; one foot planted, the other on the ball pivoting) — with a
  **swappable hand-mime** changed every 1–2 bars. Hip "pop"/crack accent on a
  strong off-beat.
- **Mime catalog (each a legible everyday action):** driving (wheel),
  boxing (jabs), ironing (push), fishing (cast+reel), phone (hand to ear) →
  **side-readable, keep**. Washing (scrub), swimming (breaststroke), praying
  (palms) → front-plane, **stylize or skip**.
- **Signature poses:** pointed-finger toe-pivot (default); fist-pop + hip-jut
  (accent); a clear mime quote (driving wheel / boxing jab).
- **2D:** unusually side-friendly — hands project forward/outward, away from the
  torso. Use the side-readable mime subset; trade mimes between cats for
  call-and-response.
- **Effort:** loose/Sustained/Free body vs. sharp **Sudden + Direct** (often
  Strong) hand-mimes — anticipation → snap → short hold per gesture.
- **Sources:** Wikipedia (Azonto); AFAR; African Music Library; DanceUs;
  DanceAsk.

## Buga — UNISON HIT (featured: chest)

- **Origin:** Kizz Daniel ft. Tekno, Nigeria, 2022. 116 BPM. "Buga" (Yoruba) = to
  show off / lift the shoulders and chest with pride. Deliberately easy; one of
  the most-imitated moves.
- **Engine:** a 4-count cell "**lo–lo–lo–BUGA**" = three prep bounces then the
  hit; the 2-bar phrase = two cells (A then mirrored A′). Hits at **fr 12 and
  28**.
- **Per-region:** *counts 1–3* — knee-dip bounce, deepest sink (anticipation) on
  count 3, arms tucked/loaded at the chest. *Count 4 (hit)* — rise to full
  height, **chest pops open**, **shoulders shrug up**, **one lead arm thrusts out
  stiff** on a forward-diagonal-up line (palm open, presenting), off-arm
  counter-posed; tail flares (follow-through); chin slightly up.
- **Signature pose:** the hit — risen tall, chest open, shoulders up, one arm
  locked out on a forward-diagonal, palm presenting.
- **2D:** single lead arm angled **into the picture plane** (not pushed at
  camera, which foreshortens to nothing); palm open so the hand reads as a shape;
  support foot down-stage.
- **Effort:** Light/Free bounces → **Strong + Direct + Sudden** hit → **Sustained
  + Bound** hold. Restrained variant exists (shoulder-only shrug) — matches the
  face-cap taste.
- **Sources:** Wikipedia (Buga song); pancocojams; Digimillennials; Vanguard.

## Pouncing Cat (Amapiano) — GLIDING CONTRAST (featured: feet)

- **Origin:** Amapiano scene, South Africa, ~2020–2023 (sub-styles: bacardi,
  sgija, zekethe; "Pouncing Cat" is a specific move). 110–115 BPM, **half-time
  feel** — the body's weight pulse lands every 2 beats (fr 0,8,16,24) while the
  feet shuffle on the 8th/16th grid. Lineage: pantsula footwork.
- **Engine:** **two nested clocks** — a slow lateral CoM glide over ~8 frames and
  fast foot-shuffle taps underneath. Pouncing-Cat variant = deep crouch + heel-toe
  switches that creep the body low and forward like a stalk. "Fast feet under a
  still, floating torso."
- **Per-region:** *feet* — lateral glide/drag (sole skims, ≤ toe-height, foot IK
  **lerps not arcs**) + heel-toe shuffle taps; the trailing foot lags ~2 frames
  (follow-through). *Knees* — constant deep-ish bend, held low. *Torso/head* —
  quiet and **level** (no bob spike); slight counter-lean against the slide.
  *Arms* — loose, passive sway lagging the CoM.
- **Signature motion (not a pose):** the low lateral foot-glide — body floats
  sideways while the trailing foot skims and the head stays dead-level. For the
  cat: the **pouncing-cat creep**.
- **2D:** foot-slides are ideal side-on; keep the glide/drag/cross/creep literal;
  stylize bacardi glute/hip work down to a faint pelvis pulse (and it's off-tone
  for suited cats anyway). Stagger the slide phase across the 3 cats for a canon.
- **Effort:** **Sustained + Bound + Light**, grounded-but-buoyant. **Minimize
  anticipation/overshoot** — use follow-through/drag only. The deliberate
  non-snappy foil to the rest of the catalog.
- **Sources:** Wikipedia (Amapiano); Splice; Vibes DJ; Bubblegum Club; Blavity;
  Jukebox Collective; Champion Rolie / Mixies Academy tutorials.

## Sekem — GROUNDED STOMP CONTRAST (featured: legs/low)

- **Origin:** MC Galaxy ("Sekem", prod. TSpize), Nigeria, **2014** (the brief's
  "2015" is close; the record is 2014). "Sekem" ≈ "to move/shift". Evolves
  Kukere/Skelewu; its foot DNA + shoulder pump feed Shakiti-Bobo → Shaku.
- **Authenticity flag:** documented Sekem is a **grounded lateral weight-shift +
  foot-twist with hands pinned front/back**, *not* a literal hard stomp. The hard
  **stomp/march + big shoulder-pump** are our deliberate (lineage-supported)
  stylization. Keep the hand pose + foot twist as the authenticity anchors.
- **Engine:** a **2-count lateral weight shift** (plant onto one side → the
  other), non-traveling. Primary keying rate = one **plant per beat** (8 plants /
  loop, alternating R-L); purist alt = one plant per 2 counts (deeper glide).
- **Per-region:** *feet* — alternating low flat-foot plants (lift ~shin-low on the
  "&", hard plant on the count) with a **foot twist/pivot** on plant. *Knees* —
  soft, always bent; absorb (squash) on impact. *Hips* — full lateral shift over
  the planting foot + slight drop on the loaded side. *Arms* — **one hand pinned
  to the chest, one to the waist/back** (anchored, not flung); can swap sides on
  the switch. *Shoulders* — **contralateral pump** (plant R foot → L shoulder
  drives down-and-forward) — the snappiest joint. *Head* — cool, steady nod;
  ears flop on impact.
- **Signature pose:** weight fully committed onto one bent-knee foot (hip dropped,
  foot just twisted), other foot peeling off, body low, **hands pinned front +
  back**, opposite shoulder pumped.
- **2D:** stomp/march, knee bounce, foot twist, **hands front/back**, and the
  shoulder jut all read cleanly in profile; re-encode the lateral weight shift as
  fore/aft rock + vertical drop, or stagger across the trio.
- **Effort:** **Strong + Sudden** on the plant/pump, **Sustained** between, **Bound**
  arms — a "press/punch" grounded contrast. Anticipation on the "&" (foot peel +
  shoulder wind-up); ground-settle follow-through (squash + ear flop) on each
  plant.
- **Sources:** The Native Mag; The Guardian NG; Wikipedia (MC Galaxy); OkayAfrica;
  Pulse NG.

## Dropped / optional

- **Dab** (Atlanta hip-hop/trap viral gesture, ~2015–2016) — **borrowed accent
  only**. Not a core Lagos/Afrobeats style. It can sit as a one-count pop-culture
  punctuation because Afrobeats club choreography did borrow global viral moves
  during that era, but it should never replace the Shaku/Zanku/Azonto engine.
  Use it as a quick diagonal hit: one forearm crosses the face, the opposite arm
  extends on a high diagonal, chest counters, then immediately recover into the
  native groove. Avoid a full dab phrase, which reads Atlanta/football/meme
  rather than Lagos.
- **Soapy** (Naira Marley, 2019) — **dropped**. Well-known meaning is a
  masturbation mime (the artist's stated prison framing); drew public
  condemnation; off-tone for suited cats. Its low-squat contrast is covered by
  Pouncing Cat and Sekem.
- **Gwara Gwara** (DJ Bongz, ZA 2016) — **optional**. Mechanically excellent for
  side-view (the propeller arm is a sagittal-plane circle; heavy Bound dip under a
  Free arm swing), but South African gqom/kwaito, not Nigerian Afrobeats. Held as
  a possible "continental breadth" flourish.
